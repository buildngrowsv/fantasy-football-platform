What Changed and Why
The previous version of Section 10A.9 treated all prop line movement as a uniform positive or negative signal and applied it directly to the CDF shift without distinguishing the reason for the move. This was a material flaw in the model.

Research into NFL betting market dynamics confirms that prop lines move for six distinct reasons, and these reasons have fundamentally different predictive value for actual player fantasy output:

	•	Sharp money (sophisticated bettors with information edges) — high predictive value
	•	Injury or depth chart news — high predictive value
	•	Weather conditions changing — medium predictive value
	•	Public money (casual bettors piling onto star players) — low predictive value
	•	Book balancing (sportsbook managing liability) — no predictive value
	•	Hype or sentiment (primetime, narrative-driven betting) — no predictive value

The v1.0 formula applied the same CDF shift weight to all of these. A +5 yard move from Sunday morning public action on a nationally televised game received the same treatment as a +5 yard move from sharp steam mid-week. This inflated projections on popular players with no underlying football justification.

THE FIX  This update inserts a Move Type Classifier between Phase 5 (CDF Construction) and Phase 6 (Adjustments). The classifier categorizes every line move before any CDF shift is applied. Each move type receives a weight multiplier that scales the signal accordingly. Public moves are heavily discounted. Sharp and injury-driven moves receive full weight.

The Move Type Classifier
The classifier runs on every prop line that has moved since the opening line. It takes four inputs and returns a move type label and a weight multiplier. This runs as a new sub-step within Phase 6 (Adjustments) before any CDF shift is calculated.

Classifier Inputs
	•	Move magnitude — yards moved since opening line
	•	Timing — day of week and hours before kickoff
	•	Bet% vs Money% divergence — sourced from tracking services (Action Network, DraftKings public data, consensus aggregators)
	•	News correlation — whether the move timing correlates with a known news event (injury report release, weather update, practice report)

Unknown Move Type
When the classifier cannot determine the reason for a move — typically overnight moves where news and betting data are incomplete — it returns a type of Unknown with a weight of 0.4x (partial application). The player card flags the move as Unverified with a note that the signal type could not be confirmed.

TIMING AMPLIFIER  All move type weights are amplified by a timing multiplier based on proximity to kickoff. A move in the final 3 hours before kickoff receives 2.0x the normal weight because late information is the most current information available. A move on the Tuesday or Wednesday before the game receives 0.7x weight because early lines are softer and subject to more correction.

Revised Composite Sharp Money Formula
The v1.0 formula was:
SharpMoney_score = (LineMovement_signal x 0.60) + (JuiceMovement_signal x 0.40)

The problem with this formula was that LineMovement_signal was applied at full weight regardless of why the line moved. The revised formula introduces the MoveTypeWeight multiplier:

SharpMoney_score = (LineMovement_signal x MoveTypeWeight x TimingMultiplier x 0.60)
                 + (JuiceMovement_signal x 0.40)

Juice movement retains its full weight and is not subject to the MoveTypeWeight multiplier. This is intentional: juice shifts are inherently a sharp signal. Sportsbooks do not adjust juice to chase public money — they adjust juice to absorb sharp action without moving the line. The juice shift is the stealth sharp money signal and should never be discounted based on the public bet percentage.

Phase 9 previously read:

Phase 9 now reads:

One additional change in Phase 9: when the classifier returns a Public move type as the primary driver of a line movement, the Market Confidence Score for that player is reduced by 0.15 (on a 0.0-1.0 scale) before the blend ratio is calculated. This causes the blend to lean slightly more on the internal model, which is not subject to public sentiment inflation.

What This Means for the Product
The move type classifier is not just a model improvement. It is a visible product feature that no competitor has. The player card now shows users not just that a line moved, but why the line moved and how much weight NovaPredict is giving the signal.

This closes the most important gap in the original design — the risk that users would see a line move labeled as a positive signal when the move was actually driven by public sentiment with no underlying football information.

	•	Sharp move up: Nova projection increases. Player card shows Sharp steam detected in teal. Users understand this is a high-confidence signal.
	•	Public move up: Nova projection is largely unchanged. Player card shows Public action in muted gray with a note explaining the distinction. Users understand we are not chasing the crowd.
	•	Injury-driven move down: Nova projection decreases. Player card shows Injury-driven move in the appropriate color. Users see the football reason immediately.
	•	Book balancing: Nova projection unchanged. Confidence interval widens. Player card shows Uncertain with a note that the market is absorbing two-sided action.

THE COMPETITIVE CLAIM  We do not just translate Vegas into fantasy points. We tell you whether to trust the signal — whether the line moved because sharp money knows something the public does not, or because casual bettors piled onto a star player before a primetime game. No other fantasy tool makes this distinction. This is the moat.

Data Sourcing for the Classifier
The classifier requires three data inputs beyond the prop line itself. These need to be sourced and integrated into the data pipeline:

1. Bet% vs Money% Data
Required to detect sharp vs public divergence. Sources:
	•	Action Network public betting splits (available via API)
	•	DraftKings public betting percentages (available on their website, scrapeable)
	•	The Lines consensus public data
Minimum threshold for sharp classification: money% and bet% diverge by more than 20 percentage points in opposite directions. This data should be ingested at the same frequency as prop lines — every 30 minutes Tuesday through Sunday morning.

2. News Event Timestamps
Required to detect injury-driven and weather-driven moves. Sources:
	•	NFL official injury report releases: Wednesday, Thursday, Friday by 4pm ET
	•	Beat reporter injury updates: monitored via Twitter/X API or Rotoworld feed
	•	Weather forecast API: Weather.com or OpenWeatherMap for all outdoor venues
The classifier cross-references the timestamp of a line move against the nearest preceding news event. If a line moves within 90 minutes of a credible injury report, it is classified as Injury/Depth Chart.

3. Multi-Book Consensus
Required to detect steam moves (simultaneous movement across multiple books). Sources:
	•	Consensus line tracking: OddsShark, DraftKings, FanDuel, BetMGM, Caesars
	•	Steam is defined as: line moves 3+ yards across 3+ books within a 20-minute window
	•	Steam is the strongest classifier for sharp action and should be flagged immediately as a user-facing alert
