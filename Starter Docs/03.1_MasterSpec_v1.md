SECTION 1  PRODUCT VISION & POSITIONING

Mission
Build the most accurate AI-powered fantasy football decision engine available to consumers. Not a rankings aggregator. Not an expert consensus tool. A true prediction system that connects Vegas intelligence, snap-level role data, season-long point budgets, and self-correcting machine learning into one unified engine.
Target User
Serious fantasy players in the top 10–15% who already understand YPRR, DVOA, and implied team totals. This app does not explain what a stat is. It uses it. Users want real data, real reasoning, and a genuine edge — not simplified takes.
Three Marketing Claims
CLAIM 1
The only app that tracks every player’s season like a financial portfolio.

CLAIM 2
Vegas knows. We translate it into your lineup.

CLAIM 3
Start/sit decisions backed by the full season, not just this week.
Core Philosophy
	•	Probability outputs, not opinion outputs
	•	The season is one continuous system, not 17 isolated weeks
	•	Market intelligence (Vegas) is the sharpest signal available
	•	Every miss is a data point that improves future predictions
	•	Uncertainty is information — show it, don’t hide it

SECTION 2  CORE ARCHITECTURE OVERVIEW

Five Engine Layers
Layer 1 — Data Ingestion
Real-time feeds: Vegas lines and props, injury reports, depth charts, weather, Next Gen Stats, snap counts, beat reporter news, official injury designations.
Layer 2 — Role & Opportunity Modeling
Snap share, route participation, target share, red zone usage, situational usage splits. Detects role changes week over week automatically.
Layer 3 — Projection Engine
Bayesian weekly update model. Outputs floor, median, ceiling, boom %, bust %, and fragility score for every player. Adjusts live for league scoring format.
Layer 4 — Season Budget System
Tracks every player’s preseason projected point pool. Updates weekly with actual performance. Flags pace deviations, regression candidates, and bounce-back targets. Feeds directly into weekly projections.
Layer 5 — Decision Engine
Produces Start/Sit recommendations, preseason rankings, and weekly rankings. Runs counterfactual scenario trees on demand. Tracks every prediction and retrains weights by failure type.

SECTION 3  LEAGUE SCORING ENGINE

Every projection, ranking, floor, ceiling, boom %, and confidence score recalculates live based on the user’s exact league settings. This is not cosmetic. It changes the math.

Scoring Format Toggles
	•	PPR (1 point per reception)
	•	Half-PPR (0.5 points per reception)
	•	Standard (0 points per reception)
	•	TE Premium (1.5 PPR for tight ends)
	•	SuperFlex (QB value increases across the board)
	•	Best Ball (ceiling weighting increases)
	•	Underdog / Snake format variants
Scoring Variable Inputs
	•	Passing TD value (4pt vs 6pt)
	•	Rushing and receiving yard increments
	•	Custom bonus thresholds: 100-yard bonus, 200-yard bonus
	•	Interception and fumble penalties
	•	Return yardage included or excluded
	•	2-point conversion value
Impact
A PPR WR2 with 8 catches for 60 yards is a top-15 play in PPR and a fringe play in standard. The engine produces completely different rankings and start/sit recommendations for each format. Trade values, waiver priority, and season budget projections all reflect the format.

SECTION 5  SEASON POINTS BUDGET SYSTEM

The Season Points Budget System is the feature that no other fantasy app offers. It treats each player’s season as a complete financial system — not 17 isolated weeks. Every weekly recommendation is informed by where a player stands against their season point budget.

Core Inputs
	•	Preseason projected total points (model consensus)
	•	Weeks played and weeks remaining
	•	Actual points scored to date
	•	Points remaining in budget
	•	Required weekly pace (points needed per remaining week)
	•	Actual weekly pace (points scored per week so far)
Weekly Outputs
	•	Points scored this week
	•	Season total to date
	•	Projected points remaining
	•	Budget status: On Pace / Ahead of Pace / Behind Pace
	•	Pace deviation percentage
Flags & Signals
	•	Hot Pace Flag — scoring significantly above required weekly pace (regression candidate)
	•	Cold Pace Flag — scoring significantly below required weekly pace (bounce-back candidate)
	•	Fluke Week Detector — single-game spike vs. season average (prevents chasing noise)
	•	Sustained Outperformance — 4+ consecutive weeks above pace triggers projection revision upward
	•	Sustained Underperformance — 4+ weeks under pace triggers projection revision downward
	•	Budget Exhaustion Warning — player on pace to exhaust season pool before Week 17
Projection Revision Triggers
	•	Injury (missed games adjust remaining pool and weekly pace requirement)
	•	Role change (snap or target share shifts 10%+ sustained over 3 weeks)
	•	Teammate injury (changes opportunity share)
	•	Trade or depth chart change
	•	QB change
	•	Coaching staff change
Important Design Note
The budget is dynamic, not a hard cap. Players can and do outperform preseason projections. The system tracks the deviation and updates the projection accordingly. A player 40% through their point budget in Week 3 is either a sell-high or a fluke — the system flags both and feeds that signal directly into the Start/Sit engine as a regression weight.
Key Insight
WHY IT MATTERS
A player who scores 30 points in Week 1 when projected for 130 on the season has spent 23% of their budget in one week. That is almost certainly not sustainable. The system flags the regression risk immediately and adjusts their weekly projection downward — while tracking whether the outperformance is real or fluky.

SECTION 7  VEGAS MODE — DEEP INTEGRATION

Vegas lines are set by the sharpest predictive models on earth with billions of dollars of accuracy incentive behind them. They incorporate injuries, weather, travel, matchup quality, pace, and game script before we ever touch the data. We translate that intelligence into player-level projections.

Pre-Game Lines
	•	Opening line vs. current line (movement direction = sharp money signal)
	•	Spread (favorite vs. underdog — game script indicator)
	•	Over/under total
	•	Implied team total (derived from total and spread)
	•	Moneyline (blowout probability signal)
Line Movement Signals
	•	Steam moves (fast coordinated movement = sharp action)
	•	Reverse line movement (public on one side, line moves other way = sharps disagree)
	•	Total movement: opening to current (3+ point move = significant signal)
	•	Team total movement (most player-relevant signal)
	•	Book disagreement score (stale book vs. sharp books)
	•	Public % vs. handle % divergence
	•	Late-week steam (Thursday–Saturday sharp action)
	•	Closing line value tracking
Player Props
	•	Receiving yards prop (consensus across all books)
	•	Rushing yards prop
	•	Receiving touchdowns prop
	•	Anytime TD scorer odds
	•	First TD scorer odds
	•	Receptions prop
	•	Carries / attempts prop
	•	Passing yards and TDs (QB props affect all pass catchers)
Prop Intelligence
	•	Cross-book prop consensus: median, high book, low book, fastest mover, stale book, disagreement score
	•	Prop line movement tracking: opening vs. current
	•	Sharp prop money detection: significant move = market knows something
	•	Prop suspended or pulled from board = injury signal (often precedes official reports)
	•	Model vs. market divergence alert: if model loves a player but props fall, force a review
Vegas-Derived Player Stat Implication
This is our most unique capability. We do not just use the implied team total as a scalar. We back-calculate player-level implied stats:

	•	Implied carries: team total + run/pass tendency + game script = expected rushing attempts distributed by backfield share
	•	Implied targets: team total + pass volume tendency + target share by player = expected targets
	•	Implied red zone trips: scoring total + historical TD-per-trip rates = expected scoring opportunities
	•	Implied passing yards: passing yards prop + distribution model = WR/TE yardage expectation
	•	Spread-to-game-script matrix: 7-point favorite in a 44-point game tells a completely different story than a 7-point favorite in a 51-point game
Game Total Environment Tiers
47+ Total
Shootout environment. Prioritize pass catchers on both teams. Elevated ceiling for all WRs and TEs.

43–46 Total
Normal balanced environment. Standard projections apply.

Under 43 Total
Low-scoring environment. Fade volume plays. Prioritize efficiency and TD equity.
Spread Environment Tiers
Favorite 7+
Run-heavy game script likely. Fade WRs on the favorite team in the 4th quarter. Elevate the RB.

Underdog 7+
Forced passing. Elevate WRs and TEs on the underdog. Watch for garbage time beneficiary.

Within 3
Balanced script. Neutral game script weighting applies.

Multiple variables stack. The output shows both the combined delta and each variable’s individual contribution.

TAGLINE
Stop guessing. Run the what-if.

SECTION 9  PROJECTION INTELLIGENCE LAYER

9A — Projection Distributions
We do not produce single-number projections. Every player has a full probability distribution.

	•	Floor (10th percentile outcome)
	•	Low median (25th percentile)
	•	Median (50th percentile)
	•	High median (75th percentile)
	•	Ceiling (90th percentile)
	•	Boom % (probability of 20+ PPR points)
	•	Bust % (probability of sub-10 PPR points)
	•	Finish top-12 at position %
	•	Week-winning score probability
Two players can have identical medians and completely different risk profiles. This is the foundation of every start/sit recommendation.
9B — Projection Fragility Score
Every projection is tagged with its primary failure mode. Two players with the same median have very different profiles if one is TD-dependent and the other is volume-based.

	•	TD dependent (score collapses without a touchdown)
	•	Long-play dependent (needs a 20+ yard gain to hit projection)
	•	Low-volume spike (high efficiency on few touches, fragile)
	•	Teammate injury dependent (projection requires a specific teammate out)
	•	Game-script dependent (only works in one game script)
	•	Negative script dependent (only good as underdog)
	•	Game stays close dependent
	•	Coverage dependent (requires specific CB matchup)
9C — Earned vs. Gifted Classification
Tags prior fantasy points to distinguish sustainable production from noise.

	•	Earned through real volume (sustainable)
	•	Earned through confirmed role change (sustainable)
	•	Gifted by broken play (one-time event, not repeatable)
	•	Gifted by fluky TD concentration (red zone luck, regress)
	•	Gifted by defensive collapse (anomaly, not repeatable)
This is the primary tool for stopping users from chasing noise after a big week.
9D — Projection Decomposition
Every player’s projected points are split into components so users can see which part is fragile.

	•	Volume points (yards and receptions from opportunity)
	•	Efficiency points (overperformance relative to opportunity)
	•	TD points (touchdown contribution)
	•	Game environment points (uplift from favorable game script and Vegas total)
	•	Market adjustment points (model revised up or down based on prop signals)
Displayed as a stacked bar so users immediately see what’s driving the number.
9E — Surprise Probability
Separate from the main projection. Answers: how likely is this player to massively over or underperform?

	•	Probability of beating projection by 30%+
	•	Probability of missing projection by 30%+
	•	Probability of posting a week-winning score
	•	Probability of being a complete bust (under 5 PPR points)
Especially useful for DFS-style ceiling decisions, underdog roster choices, and deciding between a safe and volatile option.
9F — Confidence Calibration
The system tracks whether its confidence scores are accurate over time. If 70% confidence calls win 58% of the time, the system is overconfident and adjusts.

	•	Weekly confidence score per recommendation
	•	Rolling accuracy at each confidence tier (60%, 70%, 80%, 90%)
	•	Overconfidence flag when calibration diverges
	•	Lean Only signal: recommendation is directional but below confidence threshold
	•	Abstain signal: when historical data on this scenario is too thin to project reliably
No other consumer fantasy tool shows users whether its own confidence scores are calibrated. This is a major transparency differentiator.

SECTION 10  ROLE CHANGE DETECTION SYSTEM

A human analyst notices some role changes. This system catches all of them, every player, every week, automatically. A role change is often the highest-value signal available before the market adjusts.

Detection Signals
	•	Snap count jump (10%+ increase week over week)
	•	Route participation jump
	•	First-read target jump (QB looking this player’s way earlier in progressions)
	•	Red zone role jump (new presence or expansion in red zone)
	•	Slot vs. outside alignment change
	•	Motion usage increase
	•	Teammate absence impact (who absorbs the vacated role)
	•	Two-minute drill role change
	•	Third-down role change
	•	Goal-line back status change
Implied Role Inheritance Modeling
When a player gets injured, the system does not just flag the backup. It models exactly which statistical categories transfer to which replacement based on historical snap and route data. Target share inheritance is not equal — a slot WR’s targets go to a different player than an X receiver’s.

	•	Maps vacated targets by route type to most likely inheritor
	•	Maps vacated carries by situation (early down, goal line, 3rd down) to inheritors
	•	Identifies primary and secondary beneficiaries
	•	Updates season budget projections for all affected players
Output
	•	Role change alert with severity score
	•	Which stats are affected and by how much
	•	Confidence in the role change (one week vs. trend)
	•	Season budget revision triggered automatically

SECTION 11  LEARNING & ERROR MEMORY ENGINE

Every prediction is tracked. Every miss is tagged with a reason. The model retrains its weights based on failure type, not just binary right/wrong outcomes. This is the long-term competitive moat.

Weekly Tracking
	•	Projected points vs. actual points for every player
	•	Start/Sit decision: correct or incorrect
	•	Confidence score vs. actual outcome (calibration tracking)
	•	Season budget pace vs. actual (budget model accuracy)
Error Tagging System
Every miss is tagged with its primary failure mode:

	•	TD variance (right on volume, wrong on touchdowns)
	•	Volume miss (usage was lower than projected)
	•	Snap/role miss (role change not detected in time)
	•	Injury miss (unexpected in-game injury)
	•	Coverage miss (shadow assignment not anticipated)
	•	Blowout miss (game script prediction wrong)
	•	Weather miss (weather impact over or underestimated)
	•	Market disagreement miss (props were right, model was wrong)
	•	Coaching change miss (OC/HC tendency model outdated)
	•	News credibility miss (overweighted a low-credibility source)
Wrong for the Right Reason Logging
If a player saw 12 targets and produced no TD, that is not the same miss as a volume miss. The system logs the actual usage data alongside the outcome and tags it correctly. A player who was right on opportunity but wrong on results does not trigger the same retraining as a player who was wrong on opportunity.
Weight Adjustment
	•	Each error type triggers a targeted weight adjustment in the model
	•	TD variance errors reduce TD-prediction confidence, not volume confidence
	•	Role miss errors increase snap-change detection sensitivity
	•	Market disagreement errors increase Vegas signal weighting
	•	Seasonal retraining using full prior year data
	•	Position-specific retraining (QB misses don’t affect RB model weights)
Blind Spot Log
The system maintains a running log of signal types that were absent from the model when a miss occurred. This drives feature development priorities and surfaces gaps in the data layer.
