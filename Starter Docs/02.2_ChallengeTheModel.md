Challenge the Model is a head-to-head prediction feature that lets users make their own player projections using the same underlying data the NovaPredict model uses, then compete against the model’s picks in real time. Both are locked before kickoff. After each game, performance is scored, compared, and tracked. The user who consistently beats the model is not a threat — they are the most engaged, most vocal, and most valuable subscriber on the platform.

THE CORE INSIGHT
Every other fantasy tool is afraid of being beaten. They hide their accuracy, bury their misses, and present themselves as oracles. We design the entire feature around the possibility that users will win — because a user who beats the model using our data is the best marketing we have. They can’t beat us without us.

THE THREE SUB-FEATURES

Sub-Feature 1: The Pick Slate
The Pick Slate is the weekly interface where users make their predictions. It is available from Tuesday (when projections are first published) through 90 minutes before each game’s kickoff (when picks lock).

What the User Sees
A scrollable weekly card showing every fantasy-relevant player with the NovaPredict projection displayed prominently. For each player the user has three options:

Action
Description
AGREE
User taps to confirm they agree with the NovaPredict projection. No additional input required. Logged as user_pick = model_pick for scoring purposes.
OVERRIDE
User disagrees with the projection. They enter their own PPR projection (numeric) or alternatively their own start/sit call. A required override reason is selected from a short list (see below). Logged separately.
SKIP
User chooses not to pick this player this week. Skipped players are excluded from scoring. No penalty for skipping.

Override Reason Selection
When a user overrides the model they must select one reason from a short list. This takes 2 seconds and is not a barrier — it is data. Over thousands of overrides it tells us exactly what signal types the user community is systematically identifying that the model is missing.

Override Reason
What It Signals to Us
Vegas line moved late
User saw a prop line move after our projection was published and is adjusting for it.
Injury / practice report
User has information from Friday or Saturday practice that changes the outlook.
Historical signal
User ran a Historical Intelligence Engine query and found a pattern our model underweights.
Matchup read
User believes the specific player-defense matchup is better or worse than our model implies.
Gut / other
User disagrees but does not have a specific data-driven reason. Tracked separately — these overrides tend to perform worse on average, which is useful to show users over time.

Pick Slate Mechanics
	•	Lock time: 90 minutes before each game’s kickoff. Picks cannot be changed after lock. Players who are ruled out as inactive after lock are voided and excluded from scoring for that week.
	•	Partial slate: Users do not need to pick every player. Skipped players are excluded. Minimum 5 picks required to register a scored week.
	•	Start/Sit mode: Users can optionally set the Pick Slate to Start/Sit only mode — for each pair of players they’d choose between, they pick one. Simpler engagement, slightly different scoring.
	•	Pre-filled option: Users can tap “Agree with all” to confirm all current projections in one tap. Designed for users who want to benchmark the model without engaging player by player.

Sub-Feature 2: The Score Card
The Score Card is the post-game results screen, available Monday morning after actuals are processed. It shows the head-to-head result for the week and updates the season record.

Scoring Methodology
Two parallel scoring systems run simultaneously. Both are always visible to the user.

Metric
Definition
Formula
Start/Sit Accuracy
Binary correct/incorrect for each start/sit decision. Correct = the player the user (or model) started outscored the alternative. Incorrect = the player they sat outscored the one they started.
USER_SS_PCT = correct_starts / total_decisions
MODEL_SS_PCT = correct_model_starts / total_decisions
DELTA = USER_SS_PCT - MODEL_SS_PCT
Projection MAE
Mean Absolute Error between the projection and actual PPR score. Lower MAE = more accurate projection. Measured only on players the user made a numeric projection for (Agree or Override with a number).
USER_MAE = AVG(|user_proj - actual|)
MODEL_MAE = AVG(|model_proj - actual|)
DELTA = MODEL_MAE - USER_MAE (positive = user is more accurate)

Sub-Feature 3: The Edge Finder
The Edge Finder is the most powerful sub-feature and the one most likely to create evangelical users. It runs after each week’s scoring and surfaces systematic patterns in a user’s override performance. It answers the question: in what specific situations are you consistently smarter than the model?

How It Works
The Edge Finder requires a minimum of 8 override observations in a specific category before surfacing a finding. It computes the user’s override win rate vs the category baseline (50% = matching the model’s accuracy; above 50% = beating the model in that category) and surfaces findings once statistical significance approaches.

EDGE FINDER — PATTERN DETECTED
Category:  WR2 on short weeks (Thursday game)
Your override record:  11 wins, 3 losses  (78.6% correct)
Model accuracy in this category:  61.4% correct (STRONG baseline)
Your edge:  +17.2 percentage points above model in this category
What this means:  You appear to have a systematic signal our model is underweighting on WR2 short-week games. You’re finding value the algorithm is missing.
Suggested action:  Run a Historical Intelligence Engine query to explore this further. Share your methodology with the community.

SCORING SYSTEM — FULL SPECIFICATION

Start/Sit Accuracy Scoring
Start/Sit scoring requires the user to have made explicit picks on at least two players in the same position group for a given week. A correct call is defined as the started player outscoring the benched player.

SS_CORRECT_WEEK  = count(started_player_PPR > benched_player_PPR)
SS_TOTAL_WEEK    = count(valid start/sit decisions made)
SS_PCT_WEEK      = SS_CORRECT_WEEK / SS_TOTAL_WEEK
SS_PCT_SEASON    = cumulative SS_CORRECT / cumulative SS_TOTAL

Scenario
Rule
Valid decision
User picked Player A to start over Player B in the same position. Both played at least 10 snaps. Outcome is scoreable.
Voided decision
Either player was inactive, injured out in the first quarter, or had fewer than 10 snaps. Decision is voided and excluded from scoring.
Tied decision
Both players scored within 0.5 PPR of each other. Counted as 0.5 correct for both user and model. Not a full win or loss.
Model baseline
The model’s SS% is computed using its own median projections as the start call. This is the same methodology applied identically to both user and model. No adjustment for difficulty of decision.

Projection MAE Scoring
MAE scoring is available only for users who enter numeric projections (either their own number on an Override, or for Agrees where the model projection is used as the user’s projection by default).

PLAYER_ERROR_i   = |USER_PROJ_i - ACTUAL_PPR_i|
USER_MAE_WEEK    = AVG(PLAYER_ERROR_i) for all scored players this week
MODEL_MAE_WEEK   = AVG(|MODEL_PROJ_i - ACTUAL_PPR_i|) for same players
USER_MAE_SEASON  = cumulative AVG across all scored player-weeks

	•	Minimum 5 player projections per week required for the week’s MAE to count toward season totals.
	•	Players who are inactive after lock are excluded from MAE scoring for that week.
	•	The model’s MAE uses its published pre-game median projection, not any post-lock update. Both user and model are held to the same pre-lock information standard.
	•	MAE is displayed at the position level (WR MAE, RB MAE, TE MAE) as well as overall. Users often find they’re significantly better at one position than others.

Override Win Rate Scoring
OVERRIDE_WIN     = user_proj closer to actual than model_proj  (|user-actual| < |model-actual|)
OVERRIDE_LOSS    = model_proj closer to actual than user_proj
OVERRIDE_TIE     = |user-actual| and |model-actual| within 0.5 PPR of each other
OVERRIDE_WIN_PCT = (OVERRIDE_WIN + 0.5 x OVERRIDE_TIE) / OVERRIDE_TOTAL

INTERPRETATION BENCHMARK
An override win rate of exactly 50% means the user and the model are equally accurate on overrides — which is actually impressive because the model was specifically designed to be accurate. Override win rates above 55% over 20+ observations suggest a genuine systematic edge. Above 65% over 30+ observations is exceptional and almost certainly a real model blind spot.

THE MODEL IMPROVEMENT LOOP

Challenge the Model is not just a retention feature. It is a systematic model audit run by thousands of users simultaneously. Every override with a reason attached is a hypothesis. The aggregate of winning overrides in a specific category is evidence of a model blind spot. This is the most valuable form of feedback available because it is financially incentivized — users who find real edges are winning money in their fantasy leagues, so they are highly motivated to be right.

How User Override Data Feeds Model Development
Mechanism
What Happens
Timing
Weekly: Flag emerging patterns
If > 30 users override the model in the same direction on a specific player this week, and that player outperforms the model’s projection, flag for review. This could be a shared signal the community is identifying (late injury news, line movement) that the model missed.
Automated weekly report
Monthly: Category win rate analysis
Aggregate all override win rates by category across all users. Categories where the community consistently outperforms (override win rate > 55% across 100+ observations) are model blind spot candidates.
Monthly model review
Seasonal: Coefficient recalibration
Categories with confirmed systematic community edges inform the annual coefficient recalibration. If 500 users beat the model on WR2 short-week overrides at 64%, the short-week WR2 coefficient is almost certainly wrong.
Annual offseason rebuild
User insight sessions
The top 10 leaderboard users (with their consent) are invited to an optional feedback session each offseason. They describe their methodology. This is free consulting from the community’s best analysts.
Annual, opt-in

The Flywheel
The model improvement loop creates a compounding advantage over time:

Timeline
What Accumulates
Year 1
Users override the model. Some win. Edge Finder identifies patterns. Community forms around shared competition.
Year 2
Systematic override wins inform coefficient recalibration. Model improves. Now harder to beat. Community engages more intensely because beating an improving model is more prestigious.
Year 3+
The model is the most accurate in the market because it has been audited by thousands of financially-motivated users for multiple seasons. This accuracy record is the primary marketing asset and the primary moat. It cannot be replicated without years of community engagement.

THE LONG GAME
Every other fantasy analytics tool improves its model by hiring more analysts or buying better data. NovaPredict improves its model by building a community of thousands of financially-incentivized auditors who are trying to beat it. The community’s effort to beat the model is the mechanism that makes the model unbeatable over time.

Key UX decisions:
	•	Historical signal badges appear inline when a known signal applies to a player. The user sees the same signal the model applied, so they can decide whether to agree or override with full context.
	•	The probability distribution bars (Floor / Ceiling / Boom%) are visible in the Pick Slate, not just the player card. This is the key data the user needs to make an informed override decision.

	•	Agree is the default state. Players appear with the Agree button pre-highlighted. The user must actively tap Override or Skip to change their pick. This reduces friction for casual use while keeping Override meaningful.

MONETIZATION & BUSINESS IMPACT

Why This Feature Doesn’t Need Its Own Revenue Stream
Challenge the Model does not need to be monetized separately. It is a retention and acquisition engine that makes the core subscription more valuable. Every user who engages with it has a higher LTV than one who does not, because:

	•	Their pick history lives in the platform. Season records, override history, Edge Finder badges — all of this is tied to their account. Switching to a competitor means starting from zero. This is a genuine switching cost that does not require a paywall.
	•	The competitive engagement deepens their relationship with the data. A user who has made 200 picks and tracked 12 confirmed overrides is not a casual subscriber — they are deeply embedded in the platform.
	•	The leaderboard creates aspiration that drives renewal. A user who finished 14th in the WR2 short-week specialist leaderboard in 2025 is renewing in 2026 to try to reach the top 5.
	•	The shared record cards drive acquisition at zero marginal cost. Every card shared on Reddit is an ad we did not pay for.
