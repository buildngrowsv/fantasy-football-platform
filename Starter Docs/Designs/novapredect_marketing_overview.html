The insight
The smartest room
in sports is already open.
Vegas is the smartest room in sports. Sportsbooks have billions of dollars, armies of analysts, and sharp bettors hammering their lines into shape with real money. When they set a player prop — a wide receiver's over/under at 87.5 receiving yards — that number reflects the best collective intelligence money can buy. It is not a guess. It is a priced probability, forged under financial pressure, by people whose livelihood depends on getting it right.
And every week, that intelligence was sitting completely untranslated. Fantasy players were reading beat writers. Watching highlight reels. Listening to podcasters argue about touchdowns. Meanwhile, the sharpest signal in sports was right there in the prop markets — ignored.

NovaPredict is the translation engine. We take the Vegas prop market apart down to the decimal, reconstruct the full probability distribution behind it, convert it to PPR fantasy points, and then layer our own model on top. The result is not a single projection number. It is a complete probability distribution for every player, every week — floor, ceiling, boom probability, bust probability, and a transparent breakdown showing exactly where every point comes from.
01 · INGEST
We read the full prop market
Not just the headline line. The full alt ladder — every threshold from 25 yards to 200+ — is a complete probability distribution priced by the market. We reconstruct what Vegas actually believes, not just what it advertises.
02 · CLASSIFY
We know why the line moved
Sharp steam, injury news, public money, and book balancing all move lines differently. We classify the reason before we apply the signal. Public action is suppressed. Sharp steam gets full weight. No competitor does this.
03 · LAYER
We add what Vegas can't see
Role changes. DNA volatility grades. Season budget pace. Historical signal patterns. Fourteen layers of proprietary intelligence applied on top of the Vegas baseline — producing a final number that the market hasn't priced yet.
How it works
Nine phases.
One number no one else has.
Every Thursday through Sunday, the NovaPredict engine runs a nine-phase pipeline on every fantasy-relevant player in the NFL. This is what happens before you ever see a projection.
01
Alt ladder ingestion
We pull every prop line from every major book — receiving yards, receptions, touchdowns, longest reception, combo props, head-to-head props. Not just the main line. Everything. Cross-book consensus is calculated and the sharpest book is identified.
Vegas source · real money pricing
02
Vig removal · CDF construction
The bookmaker's margin is stripped from every line. We fit a smooth probability curve through the alt ladder data points via PCHIP interpolation — producing a complete cumulative distribution function that represents what Vegas truly believes about this player's output this week.
Probability · not a single number
03
Move type classification
Every line movement is classified: Sharp Steam, Injury-Driven, Weather, Public Action, Book Balancing, or Unknown. Each type carries a different signal weight multiplier. Public moves are suppressed to 0.15×. Sharp steam and injury moves receive 1.0× full weight. The classifier runs before any CDF shift is applied.
New · no competitor does this
04
Monte Carlo simulation
10,000 correlated scenarios are drawn from the yards, receptions, and TD distributions simultaneously, accounting for statistical relationships between them. Each scenario is converted to PPR points. The output is a complete probability histogram — not a point estimate, a distribution.
10,000 simulations · per player · per week
05
Internal model + final blend
The Nova internal model runs in parallel — DNA volatility grade, season budget pace, usage trends, role changes, historical signal flags, weekly conditioning. It blends with the market distribution proportional to market confidence. Low-confidence markets lean on the internal model. Sharp-side markets lean on Vegas. Both inform the final output.
The model edge · the +3.2 pts you can't get anywhere else
06
Season budget tracking
Every player has a Vegas Season Budget — derived from preseason season-long props — and a Nova Season Budget layered on top. Each week's actual output feeds back into the remaining budget. Players behind pace have elevated boom probability. Players ahead of pace get the post-boom regression signal. The weekly card and the season arc are connected.
