Context — Why This Section Is Being Updated
The NovaPredict model now classifies the reason behind every prop line movement before applying it to the player projection. This is documented in AIRunbook Section 10A.9 v2.0 and described at a business level in the Business Overview Competitive Positioning Update.

This update to the Stickiness document revises the Section 4 Player Card component specification to reflect: (1) the new Move Type label as a required display element in the Vegas Signal section, and (2) additional components added during the UI design phase that were not in the original spec.

SCOPE  This document updates Section 4 only. All other sections of the Stickiness document — five-tab navigation, weekly content calendar, retention mechanics, social features — are unchanged.

Section 4 — Player Card: Full Updated Component List
The player card is the most-viewed screen in the app and the core unit of the product. The complete list of required components, in display order, is:

Above the Fold — Primary Screen

Header Block
	•	Player name, position, team, jersey number
	•	Matchup (opponent, day, time, location) — includes home/away designation
	•	Start/Sit tier badge (Tier 1 Start / Tier 2 Start / Flex / Sit)
	•	Vegas-Implied PPR — the fantasy point output of the raw prop baseline, displayed prominently with muted styling
	•	NovaPredict PPR — the model's projection after all adjustments, displayed as the primary number in teal
	•	Model edge badge — the delta between Vegas-implied and Nova (e.g., +3.2 model edge)
	•	Secondary stat strip — line move, game total, market confidence score

Fantasy Point Breakdown — WR/RB/QB/TE Formula
Component-by-component breakdown of how the projection was built. This is a required section and is unique to NovaPredict — no other platform shows this.
	•	TD Scoring toggle — switches TDs on/off across all numbers in real time
	•	Table columns: Component | Vegas PPR | Nova PPR | Edge
	•	Rows: all scoring components for the player's position (catches, yards, TDs, 2-pt conversions for WR/TE; rush yards, rush TDs, catches, rec yards, rec TDs for RB; pass yards, pass TDs, rush yards, rush TDs, INTs for QB)
	•	Line move sub-row indented under Receiving Yards showing the exact point value of the move (+0.45 pts from +4.5 yds)
	•	Signal Conditioning and Usage Momentum as Nova-only rows (no Vegas column value)
	•	TD Dependency Risk as a negative adjustment row
	•	Total PPR row at bottom: Vegas total | Nova total | Delta

Expected Volume
	•	Targets (with vs season avg trend)
	•	Receptions (with catch rate)
	•	Nova Receiving Yards — explicitly labeled as Nova to distinguish from Vegas prop baseline
	•	Air Yards (with team share %)
	•	TD Probability (with RZ targets per game context)
	•	Snap Share (with 4-week trend direction)

Probability Distribution
	•	Monte Carlo histogram — 10 bars spanning the full scoring range, color-coded by zone (low/mid/peak/boom). Hover on any bar to see exact range and probability percentage.
	•	Gradient distribution track below histogram — shows distribution shape
	•	Labeled percentile markers: Floor (P10) | P25 | Median | P75 | Ceiling (P90)
	•	Boom/Bust row: Boom (>=20 pts) and Bust (<10 pts) with season avg comparison and delta chip

Safety Profile
	•	DNA Grade chip (S+ through X) with grade label and sub-description
	•	Market Confidence Score bar (0-100) with classification label (e.g., Strong / Sharp-side)

Signal Flags
	•	Active signal pills (Post-Bust Bounce, High Game Total, Sharp Over, etc.)
	•	Color-coded: amber for historical/regression flags, cyan for market/game context, green for positive sharp signals

Fold Divider
A visible scroll indicator separating above-fold content from deeper analysis. Styled with arrows and a label to signal scrollability. This is not an optional UX element — the deeper analysis is critical to retention and must be discoverable.

Below the Fold — Deeper Analysis

Weekly Signal Conditioning
	•	Four cards: Boom % This Week (vs season avg) | Bust % This Week (vs season avg) | Big-Play Dependency | TD Dependency
	•	Each card shows the current week's conditioned value vs the season baseline with directional context

Active Signal Breakdown
	•	Every active signal applied to this player's projection this week
	•	Ranked by impact magnitude
	•	Horizontal bar shows relative weight
	•	Impact percentage shown (e.g., +8.2%)
	•	Green bars = positive signals | Red bars = negative signals

Usage and Role — Last 4 Weeks
	•	Three-column table: Metric | 4-week avg (with bar) | vs prior 4 weeks (trend arrow + delta)
	•	Metrics: Snap share | Target share | Air yards share | RZ targets per game

Historical Signal Record
	•	Table showing how the player has performed historically when today's active signals were present
	•	Columns: Signal description | Historical outcome
	•	Color-coded: green for favorable outcomes | red for unfavorable

Game Variables
Environmental and situational factors active for this specific game. This section was added during UI design and is a required component going forward.
	•	Each item shows: icon (positive/negative/neutral) | title | description | point impact
	•	Color-coded border and background by direction: green (positive) | red (suppressive) | amber (contextual / priced in)
	•	Footnote required for conditional items (e.g., * Conditional on player being ruled inactive)
	•	Point impacts are applied to the Nova projection where not already captured by the prop market

Vegas Signal — REVISED COMPONENT SPEC
 UPDATED   This section replaces the previous Vegas Signal spec in its entirety.

The Vegas Signal section is now structured in two layers: the fantasy point outputs at the top (primary), and the raw Vegas data below (secondary). The Move Type label is a required display element.

Layer 1 — Fantasy Point Outputs (Primary)
	•	Three-cell row: Proj PPR (from Nova, teal) | Floor PPR (P10 outcome) | Ceiling PPR (P90 outcome)
	•	Sub-label on Proj PPR: from prop line (makes the Vegas derivation explicit)
	•	Sub-label on Floor/Ceiling: P10 outcome / P90 outcome

Layer 2 — Raw Vegas Data (Secondary)
	•	Three stat cards: Prop Line (current, with opening line shown) | Line Move (in yards, with directional label) | Juice Shift (current juice, with previous juice shown)

DISPLAY RULE  The Move Type label appears in the stat card for Line Move, adjacent to the yardage delta. When the type is Public or Book Balancing, a one-line explanation note appears below the stat cards explaining why the Nova projection did not change proportionally to the line movement. This prevents user confusion when a large line move does not produce a large projection change.

Vegas Signal Text Block
Below the stat cards and above the Move Type note, a 2-3 sentence plain English summary explains the Vegas situation. This text is AI-generated weekly and must reference: the line movement amount, the move type classification, and the implication for the projection. Example:

Receiving yards prop moved +4.5 since open — sharp steam detected across three books within a 40-minute window Wednesday afternoon. Juice shifted from -115 to -130, confirming sharp-side conviction. Nova projection updated to reflect full signal weight.

Receiving yards prop moved +5.5 Sunday morning — public action identified. 78% of bets and 81% of money on the over, consistent with a primetime favorite narrative. Nova projection adjusted minimally. This move reflects sentiment, not sharp information.

Outcome Summary — New Required Section
 NEW   This section was not in the original spec. It is now required.

The Outcome Summary is the closing card on the player card. It appears at the bottom of the scroll, below Game Variables. It synthesizes everything into one card that a user can screenshot and share.

	•	Header: NovaPredict Outcome Estimate with Model Certainty badge
	•	Model Certainty: percentage (e.g., 74%) with context label (e.g., Tighter than 71% of WR projections this week)
	•	Three-cell row: Nova Projection | Vegas Implied | Model Edge
	•	Probability strip: Low (P10) | Boom % | High (P90)
	•	Verdict: 2-3 sentence AI-generated plain English summary of the recommendation, including the primary risk. Updates when TD toggle is changed.

PURPOSE  The Outcome Summary is the share card. It is designed to be screenshotted and posted. Every element is legible as a standalone image. This drives organic social sharing and is the primary acquisition surface within the app.
