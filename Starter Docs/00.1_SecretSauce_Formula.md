LAYER 01
01
Vegas Alt Ladder → Full Probability Distribution
PUBLIC

What we tell the world: We read the full Vegas prop market — not just the primary line — and reconstruct the complete probability distribution of every player's weekly outcome.

What we do
Pull primary prop lines plus full alt yard ladders (25+ through 200+ yards) across all major sportsbooks every week.
Vig removal
Strip bookmaker margin using two-sided vig removal on primary lines; book-tier formulas on alt lines. Pinnacle double-weighted as the sharpest book.
Cross-book consensus
Compute median, fastest mover, stale book, and book disagreement score. Disagreement between books is itself a signal.
Curve fitting
Fit a PCHIP smooth interpolation curve through the alt ladder data points to produce a continuous probability distribution.
Monte Carlo
Run 100,000 scenarios combining yards, receptions, and TD distributions — including correlations from combo props — to produce the final PPR output.
Outputs
Floor (P10), P25, Median (P50), P75, Ceiling (P90), Boom% (20+ PPR points), Bust% (sub-10 PPR points).


PUBLIC: Outcome — full probability distribution from the Vegas prop market. PROPRIETARY: The vig removal formula, curve-fitting parameters, and Monte Carlo correlation structure.

LAYER 02
02
Line Move Type Classifier
PARTIAL

What we tell the world: Before applying any line move to a projection, we classify WHY it moved. Sharp money gets full weight. Public sentiment gets near-zero. No competitor does this.

Six move types
Sharp Steam (1.0×), Injury/Depth Chart (1.0×), Weather-Driven (0.6×), Public/Square Money (0.15×), Book Balancing (0.0×), Hype/Sentiment (0.05×). Unknown = 0.4×.
Classifier inputs
Move magnitude, timing (day + hours to kickoff), Bet% vs Money% divergence, news event correlation.
Timing amplifier
Moves in the final 3 hours before kickoff receive 2.0× the base weight. Tuesday/Wednesday moves receive 0.7×. Does NOT apply to Public, Balancing, or Hype types.
Composite formula
SharpMoney_score = (LineMovement × MoveTypeWeight × TimingMultiplier × 0.60) + (JuiceMovement × 0.40). Juice shift always retains full weight — it is inherently a sharp signal.
Market Confidence adj.
When classifier returns Public as the primary driver, MarketConfidence is reduced 0.15 before the blend ratio is calculated, leaning output toward the internal model.
UI surface
Move type label required on every player card: Sharp steam detected (teal), Injury-driven move (teal), Weather signal (amber), Public action — low signal (gray), Uncertain (gray).


PUBLIC: The classifier exists, its six categories, and the UI labels. PROPRIETARY: Specific weight multipliers, timing amplifier values, composite formula, and confidence adjustment math.

LAYER 03
03
Vegas-Implied Stat Distribution (Back-Calculation)
PROPRIETARY

What we tell the world: We go beyond the implied team total. We back-calculate player-level implied carries, targets, red zone trips, and yardage from the full game environment.

Implied carries
Team total + run/pass tendency + game script + backfield share → expected rushing attempts distributed by player.
Implied targets
Pass volume tendency + target share per player → expected targets per player.
Implied red zone trips
Scoring total + historical TD-per-trip rates → expected scoring opportunities per player.
Implied passing yards
Passing yards prop + distribution model → WR/TE yardage expectation by player.
Game script matrix
7-pt favorite in a 44-pt game carries completely different implications than 7-pt favorite in a 51-pt game. Full spread-to-script matrix applied.
Environment tiers
47+ total: shootout (elevate pass catchers). 43–46: balanced. Under 43: low-scoring (fade volume, prioritize TD equity).


PUBLIC: That we back-calculate player-level implications from the game environment, not just team totals. PROPRIETARY: The back-calculation formulas and distribution model.

LAYER 04
04
Season Points Budget System
PUBLIC

What we tell the world: Every player's season is treated as a finite financial point pool. We track actual vs. projected production every week and carry that trajectory into every weekly projection. Vegas resets every Monday. We never forget.

Core inputs
Preseason projected total, weeks played/remaining, actual points to date, pace deviation %.
Hot Pace Flag
Player scoring significantly above required weekly pace — regression candidate. Boom% depressed.
Cold Pace Flag
Player scoring below pace — bounce-back candidate. Boom% elevated.
Fluke Week Detector
Single-game spike vs. season average. Prevents projection from chasing noise after one big week.
Sustained signals
4+ consecutive weeks above pace → projection revised upward. 4+ weeks under → revised downward.
Revision triggers
Injury (adjusts pool + pace requirement), role change (10%+ snap/target shift sustained 3 weeks), teammate injury, trade, QB change, coaching change.
Budget Exhaustion Warning
Player on pace to exhaust season pool before Week 17 — flagged as regression risk regardless of Vegas line.
Key insight
A player who scores 30 pts in Week 1 of a 130-pt projected season has spent 23% of their budget in one week. System flags this and adjusts projection while tracking whether it is real.


PUBLIC: The concept and outcomes (regression flags, bounce-back candidates, budget tracking). PROPRIETARY: The specific pace deviation thresholds, fluke detection algorithm, and projection revision formula.

LAYER 05
05
Earned vs. Gifted Score Classification
PUBLIC

What we tell the world: Not all fantasy points are equal. We tag every performance as earned through real volume or gifted by fluky circumstances. Gifted scores regress at 3× the rate.

Earned — volume
Produced through real opportunity: targets, carries, routes run. Sustainable.
Earned — role change
Confirmed snap/target share increase driving the production. Sustainable.
Gifted — broken play
One-time event not reflecting role or matchup. Not repeatable.
Gifted — fluky TDs
High TD concentration relative to volume. Regresses at 3× rate.
Gifted — defensive collapse
Anomaly defensive performance not reflective of matchup quality. Not repeatable.
Regression rate
Gifted scores downweighted at 3× rate of earned scores in following week's boom% calculation.
User-facing
Classification displayed explicitly on player card for prior 3 weeks.


PUBLIC: All five categories and the 3× regression rate — these are compelling outcome claims. PROPRIETARY: The specific scoring algorithm that determines classification thresholds.

06
Automated Role Change Detection System
PUBLIC

What we tell the world: We detect role changes automatically for every player every week — and we identify them 48–72 hours before the prop market adjusts.

Detection signals
Snap count jump 10%+, route participation jump, first-read target jump, red zone role jump, slot/outside alignment change, motion usage increase, two-minute drill role, third-down role, goal-line back status.
Target inheritance
Maps vacated targets by route type to most likely inheritor — not just the backup. Slot WR targets go to different players than X receiver targets.
Carry inheritance
Maps vacated carries by situation: early down, goal line, 3rd down — each maps to different inheritors.
Beneficiary tiers
Primary and secondary beneficiaries identified. Season budget projections updated for all affected players automatically.
Timing edge
48–72 hours before the prop market adjusts — giving users an actionable window.


PUBLIC: That the system exists, all detection signals, and the 48–72 hour timing advantage. PROPRIETARY: Specific detection thresholds and route-type inheritance mapping logic.

Learning & Error Memory Engine
PUBLIC

What we tell the world: Every miss is tagged by failure type. The model retrains differently depending on what went wrong — not just whether we were right or wrong.

Error taxonomy
TD variance, volume miss, snap/role miss, injury miss, coverage miss, blowout miss, weather miss, market disagreement miss, coaching change miss, news credibility miss.
'Wrong for the Right Reason'
If a player saw 12 targets and scored zero TDs, that is not a volume miss. The system logs actual usage alongside outcome and retrains accordingly. Critical distinction.
Targeted retraining
TD variance errors reduce TD-prediction confidence only. Role miss errors increase snap-change sensitivity. Market disagreement errors increase Vegas signal weighting.
Blind spot log
Running log of signal types absent when a miss occurred. Drives feature development priorities.
Seasonal retraining
Full prior year data used for annual model retraining.


PUBLIC: The error taxonomy and 'Wrong for the Right Reason' distinction — these are compelling transparency features worth publishing. PROPRIETARY: The specific weight adjustment formulas triggered by each error type.

STRATEGIC DISCLOSURE PHILOSOPHY
Should We Publish All of This? The Answer.

Recommendation: Be the first mover. Then be irreplaceable.
Every layer in this document will eventually be copied. That is not a reason to hide everything — it is a reason to build faster than anyone can follow.
1.  Publish the WHAT loudly. Tell users and investors exactly what outcomes the model produces. Describe what each capability does, not how it works. This builds brand authority and positions NovaPredict as the standard.
2.  Guard the HOW completely. A competitor who knows we run a Line Move Type Classifier still cannot replicate our weights, our training data, or our 15-year signal library without years of their own data collection.
3.  The real moat is data and time. Every week of live predictions, error tagging, and model retraining creates a dataset no competitor can buy. A 3-year head start in calibration history and error memory is not copyable at any price.
4.  When they copy us, they validate us. A competitor launching a Season Budget System two years after we built it is not a threat. It is proof we identified the right differentiators first — and that we have two years of real data on it.
