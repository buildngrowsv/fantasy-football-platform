This analysis evaluates the viability of an AI-powered fantasy football prediction platform that reverse-engineers Vegas sportsbook prop probability curves into player-level PPR fantasy point distributions. The platform combines Vegas-derived scoring envelopes, role distribution modeling, Bayesian weekly updating, and a Season Points Budget System to produce start/sit recommendations, preseason rankings, and full probability distributions for every player every week.

CORE THESIS
Vegas prices the full probability distribution of player outcomes every week through its alt line ladder markets. Gridiron AI strips the vig, reconstructs the curve, runs a Monte Carlo simulation, and delivers the market's own probability distribution translated into PPR fantasy points — a methodology no existing consumer fantasy tool uses.

02
PROBLEM & SOLUTION FIT

The Problem
Serious fantasy players make weekly start/sit decisions using single-number projections from tools that produce a median estimate but no probability distribution. They cannot see whether a projected 18-point player has a narrow floor-ceiling range or a wide one. They cannot see whether last week's 32-point explosion was earned through volume or gifted by fluky TDs. They have no system connecting week-to-week performance to the season trajectory. And they are not using the most accurate prediction signal available: the Vegas props market, which already prices the full distribution of every player's outcomes every week.

THE ONE-SENTENCE PROBLEM
Serious fantasy players lose real money making decisions from incomplete information when the complete picture is already priced into the Vegas props market, available to anyone who knows how to read it.

The Solution
Gridiron AI reverse-engineers the Vegas sportsbook prop market into player-level PPR fantasy point distributions. The process: ingest all available props (primary lines, alt ladders, combo props, TD props) → strip vig using two-sided and one-sided formulas → compute cross-book consensus probabilities → fit PCHIP smooth curves through the CDF data points → run 100,000-scenario Monte Carlo simulation with correlated yards/receptions sampling → output floor, median, ceiling, boom%, and bust% for every player, every week.

Adding a new sport requires new stat category definitions and role data, not re-engineering the core. Marginal expansion cost is primarily data sourcing, not engineering. Each new sport added increases LTV materially: a user subscribing for NFL + NBA has a $420 3-year LTV versus $267 NFL-only.

Model Fragility Test
SINGLE ASSUMPTION
Prop markets for individual NFL players contain accurate probability distributions that, when converted to no-vig CDFs, produce meaningfully better PPR projections than human expert consensus models. If this is false — because prop markets are too thin, biased, or insufficiently liquid for non-star players — the core value proposition degrades.

	•	Earliest observable failure signal: If prop-derived medians diverge significantly from actual outcomes in Weeks 1–3, the CDF methodology is not working as designed. This is detectable within the first 3 games.
	•	Failure mode: Gradual degradation, not collapse. The tool produces results sometimes right, sometimes wrong in unpredictable ways — indistinguishable from noise.
	•	Contingency: The internal Tier 2 model (Vegas implied totals + role distribution + efficiency adjustments) is still more sophisticated than most competitors even without the prop curve layer. The fallback produces a competitive product even if the primary thesis underperforms.
