Step 1: We Read the Vegas Prop Market — And We Know Why It Moved
Every major sportsbook prices a full set of player props for every NFL game, every week. The alt yard ladder is a complete cumulative distribution function priced by the sharpest analysts in sports with the most current information available anywhere.

NovaPredict reads that distribution, strips the bookmaker's margin, fits a smooth curve through the data points, and converts it to PPR fantasy points via Monte Carlo simulation. But we go one step further that no competitor does: we classify why the line moved before we apply it to the projection.

A line moving +5 yards because sharp money identified an edge is a completely different signal than a line moving +5 yards because casual bettors piled onto a star player the morning of a nationally televised game. The first signal is worth adjusting the projection for. The second is noise. We know the difference. We show users which one they are looking at.

THE CLAIM NO ONE ELSE CAN MAKE  Every other fantasy tool sees a line move and assumes it is informative. We evaluate it first. When Puka Nacua's receiving yards prop moves up 5 yards on Sunday morning before a primetime game, we flag that as public action and do not adjust his projection. When that same line moves up 5 yards on Thursday afternoon with sharp money on the over and the line moving against the majority of bets, we apply the full signal and alert the user. That distinction is worth real money in a fantasy season.

Updated Section: The Competitive Moat
The Business Overview contains a section describing the competitive moat. The following paragraph should be added after the existing methodological distinction language:

New Paragraph — What We Show That No One Else Shows
Every fantasy tool that uses Vegas data shows you where the line is. Some show you the line movement — how much it has moved since open. NovaPredict shows you three things none of them can: the full probability distribution derived from the prop market, the fantasy point output of that distribution component by component, and whether the line moved because sharp money knows something or because casual bettors are following a star.

That third item — the move type — is not a UX feature. It is a model output derived from comparing betting splits, juice movement, timing, and news correlation across the entire week. It runs automatically. It updates in real time. And it tells a user something that would take a professional sports bettor 30 minutes of research to determine manually.

The practical outcome: NovaPredict users do not chase inflated lines on popular players before primetime games. Competitors' users do.

Updated Section: How the Model Works — One-Paragraph Version
The following replaces the existing one-paragraph methodology description used in the About page, pitch decks, and investor materials:

NovaPredict reads the Vegas prop market for every player every week, removes the bookmaker's margin, reconstructs the full probability distribution from the alt ladder, and converts it to fantasy points via Monte Carlo simulation. We then classify why the line moved — distinguishing sharp money from public sentiment — and apply signal weights accordingly. We layer our internal model on top to capture what the market cannot see: usage trends, role changes, historical patterns, and schedule anomalies. The output is a complete probability distribution: floor, ceiling, boom percentage, bust percentage, and a transparent component-by-component point breakdown. And we publish our accuracy record every week. No other fantasy tool does any of these things.
